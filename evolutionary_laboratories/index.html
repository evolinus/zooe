The Copying Room

One shape is copied by hand, again and again. Each copy is drawn from the last one, with a small, honest mistake. Nothing is steered toward a goal — the changes simply pile up.

Press Start copying to watch generation 0 begin its journey. Each lineage starts from the exact same figure — any difference you see later came from nowhere but accumulated copying error.

A FIGURE COPY MACHINE — Fish features are independently locked inside evolutionary bounds (e.g. eye must stay in the head). Distance figures are a normalized Euclidean measure across all traits, 0.00 (identical) to ~1.00+ (unrecognizable).

The Branching Room

One ancestor, copied and mutated generation after generation, occasionally splits into two independent lineages. Neither split is steered toward any goal — each new branch just keeps drifting on its own.

Field demonstration
Divergence & speciation
gen 0 / 500 — idle
A BRANCHING COPY MACHINE — Ancestor A splits into B and C at Generation 0 (fixed). The three remaining split generations — B→D,E · E→F,G · F→H,I — are set by the sliders above and must stay in order, each at least 5 generations apart. The final lineages (C, D, G, H, I) each keep mutating independently until generation N. Every split point remains visible afterward as a frozen snapshot of the ancestor.

The Drift Room

Simulating allele frequencies in a finite population over generations. Each generation is a random sample drawn from the previous one. Over time, random variance causes alleles to drift toward fixation or loss.

THE WRIGHT-FISHER MODEL — The first 3 generations are animated in real-time, sampling individuals one by one with replacement using a roulette wheel proportional to the current allele frequency. Subsequent generations are still calculated instantly, but stepped through at about 5 generations per second so you can follow the drift. Observe how smaller population sizes (N) cause the frequency to drift more erratically, leading to faster fixation or loss.
